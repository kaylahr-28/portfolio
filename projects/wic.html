Women in Computing Around the World

March 2025

  • Figma

This website prototype highlights various women from around the world who have contributed to the field of computing. Created and presented at RIT’s first annual Women, Gender and Sexuality Studies Symposium, this group project features an interactive map that takes users to the profiles of various notable women from the country selected.

To Website

The Process

For this project, my two groupmates and I used Figma to design our prototype’s layout. Our project’s purpose was to educate others about women who have made an impact on the field of computing in an engaging way. We began by finding a color palette that would appropriately convey our goal. Then, we did research on different countries and their percentages of women who pursue computer science degrees in college. From there, we searched for specific women from these countries that have engaged in the world of computing in impactful ways. We created a template for the biography pages and applied it for all the women we researched. The main feature of our prototype is a world map that, when hovered over at designated countries, reveals the country name. When clicked, the user is led to a biography page on a woman in computing from that country. Throughout this project, I learned how to effectively engage an audience using UI features.

In December 2025, I took this prototype to the next level and developed it into a website as my own personal project, linked above.
